# Difficulty Levels

Difficulty describes the expected cognitive and technical effort of a module or
activity.

Difficulty is distinct from curriculum level. A Beginner module may contain a
challenging activity, while an Expert module may contain a simple orientation
exercise.

## Level 1 — Introductory

Characteristics:

- No prior topic knowledge required
- Strong guidance
- Minimal mathematics
- Small code examples
- Immediate feedback
- One concept at a time

Typical activities:

- Definitions
- Basic visualizations
- Short guided circuits
- Simple measurements

## Level 2 — Foundational

Characteristics:

- Basic prerequisite knowledge
- Moderate guidance
- Essential mathematics
- Multi-step examples
- Small independent exercises

Typical activities:

- Gate transformations
- Probability calculations
- Basic circuit construction
- Simulator execution

## Level 3 — Applied

Characteristics:

- Multiple concepts combined
- Reduced guidance
- Independent implementation
- Debugging and interpretation required
- Framework-specific knowledge may be needed

Typical activities:

- Algorithm implementation
- Noise experiments
- Framework comparison
- Practical optimization

## Level 4 — Advanced

Characteristics:

- Complex mathematical or engineering concepts
- Open-ended design decisions
- Performance and trade-off analysis
- Hardware-aware implementation
- Significant independent work

Typical activities:

- Variational algorithms
- Error mitigation
- Benchmark design
- Architecture analysis

## Level 5 — Research

Characteristics:

- Original investigation
- Scientific methodology
- Literature review
- Reproduction or extension of published work
- Explicit uncertainty and limitations
- Reproducible reporting

Typical activities:

- Paper reproduction
- Novel benchmark
- Experimental methodology
- Comparative research

## Difficulty Declaration

Every module, exercise and challenge should declare a difficulty value from 1
to 5.

The declaration must reflect actual effort rather than the importance of the
topic.
